San Francisco · Originally from Boston

I am a teacher, a maker of images, and a student of slow living.

Hi, I'm Sophia. I grew up in Boston and spent years living fast in New York City before something finally made me slow down. That something was yoga — my first class at Y7 in NYC, which I walked into half out of curiosity and half out of necessity. I never really left the mat after that.

I'm now based in San Francisco, teaching yoga, shooting portraits and brand work, and helping wellness creatives find the visual identity that feels unmistakably like them. I hold a 200-hour teacher training from Down Under School of Yoga, and this June I'll be completing my Restorative Yoga certification here in the city.

My photography grew from the same impulse as my practice — a desire to slow down and actually see. Natural light, quiet moments, the texture of a life lived intentionally. I bring that same unhurried attention to every shoot, whether it's a brand session or an intimate portrait.

When I'm not on the mat or behind a lens, I'm writing on Substack, reading something that challenges me, or looking for the best coffee in whatever neighbourhood I'm in.

What I believe in

A few true things

Slowness

Doing less, more fully. Presence over productivity.

Rootedness

Returning to the body, to the earth, to what is real.

Beauty

Not as performance, but as a way of paying attention.

Honesty

In teaching, in images, in the stories we tell about ourselves.

Community

We heal in relation. The mat, the studio, the table — all containers.

Practice

Not mastery, but showing up daily with curiosity and kindness.
